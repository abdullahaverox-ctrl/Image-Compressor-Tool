About This Website

Image Tools is a free online toolkit that helps you optimize images right from your web browser. With just a few clicks you can compress large photos, resize them to specific dimensions, and (soon) convert between popular formats such as JPG, PNG, and WebP. There is nothing to install and no account is required.

Image optimization matters more than ever. Heavy images slow down websites, eat into mobile data plans, and hurt search engine rankings. Compressing and properly resizing your images can dramatically speed up page loads, improve user experience, and reduce storage costs. Whether you are a blogger preparing photos for a post, an online seller listing products, a designer sharing mockups, or a student attaching images to an assignment, smaller and well-sized images simply work better everywhere.

Every tool on this site is completely free and runs entirely inside your browser using the HTML5 Canvas API. That means your images are never uploaded to a server and we do not store, copy, share, or even see your files. Processing happens locally on your device, so the tools are fast, private, and safe to use even with sensitive or personal photos. There are no watermarks, no signups, no daily limits, and no hidden costs — just simple, reliable image tools that respect your privacy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is image compression?

Image compression is the process of reducing the file size of an image without removing it from your screen or making it unrecognizable. Lossy compression (used for JPG and WebP) removes small visual details that the human eye barely notices in order to shrink the file dramatically. The result is a much lighter image that loads faster on websites, sends faster over email or chat, and takes up less space on your device.

Will image quality reduce?

A small amount of quality loss is normal when using lossy formats like JPG or WebP — that's exactly how they save space. In most cases the difference is invisible at typical viewing sizes. You can control how much quality is preserved with the quality slider in the compressor: higher values keep more detail at the cost of file size. If a particular setting reduces quality too much, just lower the compression and try again.

Is this tool free?

Yes. All tools on Image Tools are 100% free to use, with no signup, no trial period, and no daily limits. There are no watermarks added to your images and no premium tiers — every feature is available to every visitor.

Are images stored or saved?

No. Your images are processed locally inside your web browser using the Canvas API. Files are never uploaded to a server, never stored in any database, and never shared with third parties. Once you close the page, the image is gone from this site entirely. This makes the tools ideal for sensitive or personal photos.
